ℹ️ Why a 1/8" black border? (and the numbers this tool uses)

Card cutters (guillotines, print shops, or your own hand with a blade) are never perfectly aligned. A solid 1/8" (0.125") buffer on every side means a slightly-off cut trims into the border instead of leaving a white sliver or slicing the art. Real Magic cards are black-bordered, so a miscut into black is invisible.

Sizes: a card trims to 2.5 × 3.5 in (63 × 88 mm), which is 750 × 1050 px at 300 DPI, the practical minimum for a crisp print. Add a 1/8" border on each side and the file becomes 2.75 × 3.75 in = 825 × 1125 px. This tool sizes the border as a true 1/8" relative to your image, so it stays correct whatever resolution you feed it.

Resolution: under ~200 DPI (roughly below 500 px wide) prints blurry; 300 DPI is the baseline; 600 DPI (1500 × 2100) is excellent. Upscaling a small image doesn't add real detail.
